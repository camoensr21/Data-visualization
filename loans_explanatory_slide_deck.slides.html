How Credit score and Income Range combine together can influence a Loan Status¶

How the colinearity between Credit score and Credit score range affect the interest rate¶

Income Range and employment status are the two main factors that explain the difference observed in the original loan amount.¶

Prosper Loans Data exploration¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others. The data set can be found from the udacity server here https://www.google.com/url?q=https://s3.amazonaws.com/udacity-hosted-downloads/ud651/prosperLoanData.csv&sa=D&ust=1554486256021000

for the purpose of the project, I will consider 14 features : CreditGrade,LoanStatus,Borrower APR, LenderYield,ProsperRating,EmploymentStatus,ListingCategory,IsBorrowerHomeowner,CreditScorRangeLower,CreditScoreRangeUpper,AmountDelinquent,IncomeRange,LoanCurrentDaysDelinquent.

Most variables are numeric (continuous and discrete) and some are categoric.

  • numeric continuous variables: Borrower APR,LenderYield,CreditScorRangeLower,CreditScoreRangeUpper,AmountDelinquent, LoanOriginalAmount, LoanCurrentDaysDelinquent.
  • categoric variables: 1- ordinal : CreditGrade, ProsperRating, IncomeRange 2- nominal : LoanStatus, EmploymentStatus, ListingCategory, IsBorrowerHomeowner

Summary of findings¶

The findings are organized through three main questions :

Question 1: What factors affect a loan’s outcome status?

  1. Relationship between LoanStatus and Income Range: We noted there's a difference between the completed and the current status, for the current the income range which is most important is higher than the one for completed; It exists the same trend in the way the Income Range is distributed for current, tradeoff and defaulted loans.
  2. Relationship between LoanStatus and CreditScoreRangeUpper: We noted some outliers for Completed, Defaulted and Chargedoff Loan status, while there are no outliers for cancelled Loan; We noted as well that the IQ range is larger for cancelled loans.

CreditScoreRange and IncomeRange have strong relationship, putting together they strengthen their impact on loan status,that could be considered as a colinearity effect.

Question 2: What factors affect the borrower’s APR or interest rate?

  1. Relationship between APR and Lender Yield I found that it exists a strong relationship between Borrower APR and Lender Yield. More the annual percentage rate is high more will be the earnings of the lender.
  2. Relationship between APR and Credit score range The APR is affected by the Creditc score range, the lower your Credit score is the higher is your APR.

Credit score are linked to credit score range, which combined together have a strong impact on APR

Question 3: Are there differences between loans depending on how large the original loan amount was? I was interested in looking at some key factors combined together which can explain the differences in the original loan amount.

  1. Relationship between original Loan amount and credit score range The original loan is affected by the Credit score range,the better is the Credit score, larger can be the amount of your original loan.
  2. Relationship between original Loan amount and Income Range, Listing Category and employment status There are differences between loans depending on the category for the loan. The original loan amount is affected by the income range. The higher is your income range the higher can be the original loan amount.
  3. Relationship between Loan amount and Income Range, Listing Category and employment status¶ Original loan amount is impacted by the employment status.
